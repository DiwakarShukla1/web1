Analytical Services: Understanding the Chemistry behind the Biology!

IMTTL is the Analytical Division of our company. We have 5 top of the line laboratories spread over India which has been providing soil, tissue and water analysis to the agricultural sector.

AT IMTTL WE BELIEVE THAT SOIL ANALYSIS SHOULD BE REALISTIC, HOLISTIC AND DETERMINISTIC! Soil analysis is a tool that has been employed with the primary goal of providing recommendations for soil rectification, crop productivity and/or soil health management. Conventional methods Diethylene Triamine Penta Acetic Acid and ammonium acetate extraction are commonly used for analysis of bioavailable nutrients. However, there are limitations to these methods as both extraction fluids are buffered to neutral or near-neutral pH. Hence extracted nutrients represent a “potential or ideal-case” fertility status of soil instead of an “actual” field status.

IMTTL ANALYSIS: pH as the Yardstick for Analysis : pH is called the master variable in the soil which means it is THE controlling factor as far as nutrient solubility and plant availability of nutrients in the soil is concerned. Ionic activity (supply of nutrients to roots) as well as the “cation and anion ionic capacity” (the release of nutrient ions from a fixed form to available form) are both pH dependent. Hence, the validity and agronomic dependence on the pH chart. With the exception of phosphorus, which has a small available pH range, macronutrients (N, K, Ca, Mg, and S) are more available within a pH range of 6.5 to 8, while the majority of micronutrients (B, Cu, Fe, Mn, Ni, and Zn) are more available within a pH range of 5 to 7. This trend should reflect in all soil audits and hence a modified method in which the extraction fluid is buffered to actual soil pH is used at IMTTL to get a REALISTIC snapshot of the soil. For ACIDIC soils we use the MEHLICH 3 reagent for extraction of nutrients and finally adjust extract to pH of the soil in question. For ALKALINE soils we use the DTPA/Ammonium Acetate reagents for extraction of nutrients and finally adjust extract to pH of the soil in question

The following parameters are included in our comprehensive Soil Audit:

    2. Crop Productivity Soil Analysis: N,P,K, Ca ,Mg, S, Fe, Mn, Zn, Cu, B, CEC, % Base Saturation. Aluminium is included as a parameter for acidic soils.
    3. Microbiological Enumeration.
    4. Biochemical Parameters:
      1. Urease Activity
      2. Phosphomonoesterase Activity
      3. Fluorescein Diacetate Activity
    5. Detailed and Customized Recommendations based on crop and soil in question.
    6. Photo File of microbial plates for the soil in Question.
